Rent the Scooter!

**The Best Decision I Made at Disney: Why I Ditched Walking for My New Best Friend!**

I just returned home from a **WONDERFUL** , four-day trip to Walt Disney World Resort with my fellow travel advisor, Karen Musgrave. We followed that truly magical stay with a three-night cruise on the Disney Wish!

And let me just say, if I **HAD NOT** decided to rent an ECV (Electric Conveyance Vehicle) for our time at Walt Disney World, there are two things I know for sure:

  1. I would never have made it through the parks.

  2. I would have been so miserable after four days that I would have spent the entire cruise in bed and missed all the fun!

![IMG_5658.JPEG](https://storage.googleapis.com/production-gator-v1-0-8/048/1869048/dlLTFCpF/c665b994c53c4dc08c05862c31523c66)

**This beauty was my new best friend!**

****

Because I made the smart decision to rent this life-saver—after trying (and failing!) to walk all of Magic Kingdom on our first afternoon—I can tell you truthfully: It was the best decision I've ever made. It saved the magic we had planned at the parks and allowed me to genuinely enjoy our cruise.

**No Shame, Only Smart Choices**

****

Believe me, there is**NO SHAME** in doing what you need to do to fully enjoy the vacation you saved for, planned for, and looked forward to!

As a "Disney After 60" guru, I initially didn't want to admit that my body could no longer handle the sheer number of physical steps it takes every day at Walt Disney World. It took just six hours and 16,000 steps to convince me it was time for a **"new way to Disney."**

![waiting-for-the-bus-with-other-ecvs.JPG](https://storage.googleapis.com/production-gator-v1-0-8/048/1869048/dlLTFCpF/014b71792d15414d9266033578adf960)

Maneuvering "my new best friend" was my biggest concern. To my surprise, getting around was incredibly easy!

  * I could drive it in the resort lobby and dining areas with no problem.

  * It fit easily in our hotel room. I plugged it in nightly and never once had it run out of power in the parks.

  * Getting on and off the bus transportation was simple as well. All the drivers were so kind and helpful! I think there were only two times I had to wait for a second bus. (Most Disney buses can only hold two ECVs, though a few of the newer, bigger ones can accommodate three or four.)

**The Best Way to Rent (and Save Money!)**

****

**Cost, you ask?** If you rent for your entire stay, you will save about one-third of the cost you would pay for daily rentals inside the parks.

For my four-day rental, I paid a total of **$189.00** , which included a $24 damage waiver. That breaks down to about **$47.25 per day**.

**Here is my key piece of advice:**

**_Rent from the Official Scooter Rental Provider, ScooterBug._ **They will deliver the ECV directly to your resort's Bell Services/Concierge and have it waiting for your arrival. They will also pick it up at the end of your stay. The beauty of renting with them is that you don't have to meet anyone in person for the drop-off or retrieval.

Please feel free to email me at [diane@disneyafter60.com](mailto:diane@disneyafter60.com) if you would like a complimentary quote for scooter rental. I am happy to handle that accommodation for you!

**The Pitfall of Park Rentals**

****

Renting directly at any of the WDW parks is an option, but you will pay $65 per day plus a refundable deposit. More importantly:

  * The ECVs **cannot be taken off the property** of the park where you rent them.

  * While Guest Services will give you a voucher if you are park hopping, there is **no guarantee** an ECV will be available once you arrive at the next park. Park rentals are strictly first-come, first-served.

  * For many people who need an ECV, just getting from the transportation center (bus, Monorail, or Skyliner) to the park gates is a challenge!

**I highly recommend renting one that is delivered directly to your resort!** Do yourself a favor, embrace the smart choice, and save your energy for the true Disney magic.

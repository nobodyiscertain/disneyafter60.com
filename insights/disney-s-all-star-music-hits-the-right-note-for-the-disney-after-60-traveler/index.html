Disney's All-Star Music Hits the Right Note for the Disney After 60 Traveler

****

The Smart Stay: Why Disney's All-Star Music Hits the Right Note for the Disney After 60 Traveler

****

Let me start off by saying when my fellow travel advisor friend and I decided to take a business trip to Walt Disney World, we thought it would be fun and informative for our followers if we pursued this trip as “Disney on a Shoestring”. That meant we were going to have to find the most affordable means to enjoy all things Disney. That is no small task when you are accustomed to staying in occasional moderate, but mostly deluxe resorts at Disney. —and I must admit, making the switch to a Value resort was a little scary!

I hadn't stayed at a Value resort in at least 25 years. My previous experience wasn’t "up to my expectations," to say the least, so I really had to open my mind to this new experience. We chose Disney’s All-Star Music Resort as our home base for four nights, specifically looking for a clean, comfortable place to sleep that maximized our budget for park time and dining.

The $50 Lesson: The Preferred Room is Necessary

****

We originally booked a standard room, aiming for the most economical choice. However, upon arrival, we discovered our room was in the very furthest building from the main hall and the bus stops—a daunting hike for anyone, especially after a long day in the parks. We immediately had second thoughts!

We called the front desk, and they graciously found us a room in Building 1. We paid a nominal fee for the upgrade to a Preferred Room category, and let me tell you, it was worth every penny for the sheer convenience of being close to the food court and park transportation! (It was only about $50/night for the upgrade!)

The Essential Takeaway for Disney After 60 Travelers: If you are older, like us, and every step counts, the Preferred Room upgrade is non-negotiable. It is the single best way to maximize your comfort and save energy for the parks. Don’t start your day with a half-mile hike to the bus stop!

Transportation: High Marks for Efficiency****

****

My next big concern was transportation. I worried the buses would be overcrowded or require long waits. We had absolutely no problem hopping on buses to get to the park of our choice.

We traveled during a slightly slower period, and even on the crowded evening after Mickey’s Not-So-Scary Halloween Party, we got on a bus from the Magic Kingdom back to the resort with virtually no waiting. The bus service here truly shines, especially with the reduced walking from a Preferred Room.

****

Recreation: A Focus on Tranquil Relaxation

****

The theming here is fantastic, but since we were seeking quiet downtime, we focused on the amenities geared toward adults:

  • The Piano Pool: We highly recommend skipping the energetic Calypso Pool (which is where most of the kids congregate) and heading to the Piano Pool. Located in the Piano section, this smaller, grand piano shaped pool is ideal for a quieter, more tranquil swim and poolside experience.
  • Singing Spirits Pool Bar: This is a major highlight. Located outside Melody Hall, it opens at noon and stays open until at least 11:00 PM. It’s the perfect place to grab a specialty cocktail, glass of wine, or a cold beer to enjoy by the pool or take back to your room after a long day.
  • Note’able Games Arcade: If you’re looking for a quick activity, the arcade in Melody Hall features fun retros like Pac-Man and Space Invaders—a nice nostalgic touch.

The Intermission Food Court: Value Dining Success

****

There are no table service restaurants at Value Resorts. We found this to beabsolutely no problem whatsoever for our adult trip.

The food at the Intermission Food Court was surprisingly delicious and incredibly convenient. They serve all three meals, and there’s a great "grab and go" section as well.

We were impressed with the variety of healthy choices. For dinner, they feature entrees such as the incredible Mediterranean Meatball Bowl! Since I have been following the Mediterranean Diet philosophy, I ordered it. OMG! It was a plant-based "Impossible Meatball" dish, but it was absolutely delicious and a great find at a Value resort.

****

A Word of Warning: The Signature-Blend Burger

The one thing I will warn you about, both in the parks and at the food court, are the burgers! UGH!!! Read the description carefully! If it says "Grilled Signature-blend burger," BUYER BEWARE!! My friend couldn't eat hers; it was a gray, prefabricated patty that didn't resemble beef in any form or fashion. Stick to the pizza, pasta, or the unique specialty bowls or the nightly specials.

Room Comfort: ECV Friendly and Cushy

****

Our room was clean, comfortable, and, importantly for those with mobility aids, roomy enough to fit theECV I had rented into the corner to charge at night.

I slept on the Murphy Bed (the kind that pulls down from the wall with the table folding underneath it). I can report it was just as comfortable as any standard bed. The pillows were wonderfully plump and cushy, too!

****

Final Verdict: The Value is Real!

****

For adults traveling without children, if your goal is tomaximize your budget for park tickets and dining experiences while prioritizing a clean, comfortable place to sleep and a convenient bus ride, All-Star Music is a wonderful, budget-friendly choice. _Just make sure to spring for that Preferred Room upgrade!_

To Scoot or Not To Scoot

A trip to Walt Disney World is often viewed as the ultimate vacation, a place where dreams come true. But let's be honest, for many of us, especially those over 60, the magic can quickly turn into a marathon. The sheer scale of the parks, the endless walking... it can transform a dream vacation into a physical endurance test.

But what if I told you there's a simple secret to a more comfortable, joyful Disney trip? It's all about using an Electronic Conveyance Vehicle (ECV), or scooter, as a strategic tool. Hear me out. Renting an ECV isn't a compromise or a sign of weakness—it’s an investment in your stamina and, more importantly, in your magical moments. It's a tool that gives you back your freedom and confidence, allowing you to truly enjoy the parks.

The best way to explain this isn't through facts and figures, but through a story. _My story._ And specifically, the story of my big, burly Cowboy of a husband. In October of 2021, we embarked on my ultimate Bucket List Dream Vacation with our children and grandchildren. What more could a Nana ask for?! The trip started with all the excitement you can imagine. The Cowboy and I headed to Animal Kingdom upon our arrival to get a few hours in before the rest of the family flew in from California.

We had a great night once they all arrived and got settled in. Everyone was filled with excitement for the next morning. My son and grandson agreed to get up with me and rope drop Magic Kingdom the next morning. The rest of the clan planned to meet us later in the morning.

So, off we go! When the rest of the family caught up with us, PopPop was NOT with the group! My heart sank! They said he was not feeling well and would catch up with us for our dinner reservations! WHAT?!?!

This became the routine and it was tough to see. If he came to the parks, he would only make it a couple of hours before he had to return to the hotel, missing out on so much of the fun.

On the next to the last day, the kids staged a loving intervention. They told PopPop they were going to rent him an ECV so that he could spend the entire day with us and enjoy EPCOT’S Food & Wine Festival. You can imagine the major GRUMBLINGS! My stubborn cowboy wasn’t going to give in easily, but he finally caved, muttering that he and I could share it so that "I wouldn’t be so exhausted." (I knew he was just trying to save face, bless his heart.)

Resisting the ECV led to him missing out, and frankly, it took a toll on the whole family's enjoyment. We all wanted to be together, making memories. But once he got on that scooter, it was like a complete transformation. He was able to go at his own pace, and the focus shifted from managing fatigue to simply having fun. The emotional core of this lesson is simple, and it's one you should carry with you: it is always "better to ride in comfort than to power through pain."

How an ECV Reimagines Your Vacation

It’s hard to believe, but a typical day at Disney involves walking 10-14 miles! I know I was feeling it after day one, and for my husband, it was just too much. An ECV fundamentally changes this dynamic…

With a scooter, a guest can easily keep up with their family, just like my husband did on that last day. He had the stamina to stay for the late-night parades and fireworks—the very moments we thought he’d have to miss. The ECV becomes a tool for maximizing enjoyment and creating those magical moments that would otherwise be inaccessible.

Rent at the parks vs Off-Site vendors

****This is where I’m going to put on my _Travel Agent Hat_. There are many choices when it comes to renting an ECV. The first of which is renting at the parks at guest services. If you are only going to be in the parks for a day or two, you might consider simply renting a scooter once you arrive for the day. Just be aware that rentals are first-come, first served and when they are gone they are gone! Park rentals are $65/day + a refundable deposit. You cannot take the scooter out of the park. If you are park hopping, they will give you a voucher to take to the next park to grab a scooter for no extra charge. The problem with that is, they are usually gone by afternoon when you park hop.

If you are going to be at Walt Disney World for multiple days, it only makes sense to rent from an outside company for half the price that will deliver to your resort and pick up after your stay from your resort. That way you have your ECV at the resort and can take it anywhere you want to go since they are accommodated on all modes of Disney transportation.

I am not going to list all the different companies that rent ECV’s, strollers, wheelchairs, etc to guests at Walt Disney World. I am going to give you _my travel agent experience_ for handling rentals of this nature for my travelers using the “Disney Featured Provider”, Scooterbug. Scooterbug is the ONLY company that can not only deliver their products to all resorts within Walt Disney World, they can leave them with the Luggage Concierge, where you can pick it up at your convenience upon arrival to your hotel. You can also drop it back off upon departure and they will store it until Scooterbug arrives to pick it up. All the other companies must have someone 21 years or older meet them for delivery and pick up.

I have a business trip to the Most Magical Place on Earth in 10 days and will have my ECV delivered by 10:00 am so that when I arrive at the resort, I can grab the ECV, dump my luggage with the Bell Hop and get on a bus to head for our first park and enjoy our day!

My husband’s story is the perfect example of why it's always "better to ride in comfort than to power through pain." What started as a physical struggle became a powerful lesson for our entire family. By embracing a simple tool—the ECV—we were able to trade exhaustion for joy, and a trip full of missed moments became a vacation where we were all together. This experience taught us that a little planning can make all the difference, ensuring every member of your family gets to be a part of the adventure, from rope drop to fireworks.
